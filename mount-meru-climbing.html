Mount Meru Climbing - Signature Safari

At a height of 4,566 meters (14,980 ft), Mount Meru is a dormant stratovolcano located in Arusha National Park, 70 Kilometres (43 miles) west of Mount Kilimanjaro in the country of Tanzania. Typically Mount Meru Climb takes four days, but it can be done in 3 days to complete a trek to the Summit. It is visible from Mount Kilimanjaro on a clear day, and is the fifth-highest mountain in Africa, dependent on definition. Climb Mount Meru, Second highest Mountain in Tanzania, is great with no traffic and amazing wildlife experience as you hike past wild animals.

Most popular questions on climbing Mount Meru

1. How difficult is it to climb Mount Meru?

The climb is considered challenging but doable for people with good fitness levels. It involves a steep ascent and some scrambling but doesn't require technical climbing skills.

2. How long does it take to climb Mount Meru?

The trek typically takes 3 to 4 days. Most itineraries are designed to help with acclimatization and ensure a safer ascent.

3. What is the best time of year to climb Mount Meru?

The best times to climb are during the dry seasons, which are from June to October and from December to February. During these periods, the weather is more predictable, and the trails are in better condition.

4. Do I need a guide to climb Mount Meru?

Yes, climbers are required to have a licensed guide. This is for safety reasons and to comply with regulations set by Arusha National Park.

5. What kind of gear do I need for the climb?

Essential gear includes sturdy hiking boots, warm clothing (as temperatures can drop significantly at higher altitudes), a good quality sleeping bag, trekking poles, rain gear, and a daypack. Additional items like a headlamp, water bottles, and snacks are also recommended.

6. How does Mount Meru compare to Kilimanjaro?

Mount Meru is often considered a good acclimatization hike for those planning to climb Kilimanjaro. While shorter and slightly less high (4,566 meters compared to Kilimanjaro's 5,895 meters), Mount Meru offers its own set of challenges and spectacular views.

7. What are the accommodation options on the trek?

Trekkers stay in mountain huts provided by the park. These huts are basic but provide shelter and a place to sleep.

8. Are there any health considerations I should be aware of?

Acclimatization is crucial to avoid altitude sickness. It's recommended to ascend slowly and stay hydrated. Some climbers might consider taking altitude sickness medication as a precaution.

9. What wildlife might I see on the trek?

Mount Meru is located in Arusha National Park, so trekkers often see a variety of wildlife, including buffalo, giraffes, colobus monkeys, and a variety of birds.

10. Is a permit required to climb Mount Meru?

Yes, climbers need a permit, which is usually arranged by the tour operators. The cost of the permit is included in the overall trek package.

11. Can I climb Mount Meru solo?

While solo climbs are not allowed due to safety regulations, solo travelers can join group treks organized by tour operators.

12. How do I prepare physically for the climb?

Preparation should include regular cardiovascular exercise, strength training, and practice hikes. It's also beneficial to hike at altitude before attempting the climb to help with acclimatization.

13. What is the summit day like?

The summit day is typically the most challenging part of the trek, starting around midnight to reach the summit by sunrise. The final ascent involves steep climbs and some scrambling over rocky terrain.
