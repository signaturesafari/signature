Rwanda Safari Packages

Rwanda, known as the "Land of a Thousand Hills," offers a unique and unforgettable Rwanda safari experience. This vibrant country is home to lush landscapes, diverse wildlife, and a rich cultural heritage. From trekking with majestic mountain gorillas in Volcanoes National Park to exploring the sweeping savannahs of Akagera National Park, a Rwanda safari promises an adventure of a lifetime. Discover Rwanda’s stunning biodiversity, where every journey through its breathtaking terrain unveils new wonders and memories to cherish forever.

Top Safari Destinations in Rwanda

1. Volcanoes National Park

  • Gorilla Trekking: Embark on an incredible journey to see the endangered mountain gorillas in their natural habitat. This once-in-a-lifetime experience allows you to get up close with these gentle giants.
  • Golden Monkeys: Discover the playful golden monkeys, another rare primate species found in the bamboo forests of the park.
  • Dian Fossey's Legacy: Visit the Karisoke Research Center, established by Dian Fossey, to learn about her groundbreaking work in gorilla conservation.

2. Akagera National Park

  • Big Five Safari: Explore Rwanda’s only savannah park and witness the Big Five—lions, elephants, buffalos, leopards, and rhinos.
  • Bird Watching: With over 500 bird species, Akagera is a paradise for bird enthusiasts.
  • Boat Safaris: Enjoy a boat safari on Lake Ihema, where you can spot hippos, crocodiles, and a variety of water birds.

3. Nyungwe Forest National Park

  • Chimpanzee Tracking: Meet our closest relatives, the chimpanzees, in the dense, ancient rainforest.
  • Canopy Walk: Experience the thrilling canopy walkway, offering breathtaking views of the forest and its inhabitants.
  • Primate Paradise: Nyungwe is home to 13 primate species, including colobus monkeys and L'Hoest's monkeys.

Unique Experiences

  • Cultural Encounters: Engage with local communities and learn about Rwanda’s vibrant culture and history through traditional dance, music, and crafts.
  • Lake Kivu: Relax by the shores of Lake Kivu, one of Africa’s Great Lakes, and enjoy water activities such as kayaking, boat tours, and fishing.
  • Kigali City Tour: Explore Rwanda’s capital, Kigali, a city known for its cleanliness, safety, and rich history. Visit the Kigali Genocide Memorial for a poignant reminder of the country’s past and its incredible journey of recovery.

Sustainable Tourism

Rwanda is a global leader in conservation and sustainable tourism. Efforts to protect endangered species and habitats are integrated into the safari experience, ensuring that your visit contributes to the preservation of this stunning natural environment.

Here are some popular questions often asked by travelers planning a safari in Rwanda

1. When is the best time to visit Rwanda for a safari?

The best time to visit Rwanda for a safari is during the dry seasons, from mid-December to early February and from June to September. These periods offer the best conditions for gorilla trekking and wildlife viewing, with less rainfall and easier hiking conditions.

2. What should I pack for a safari in Rwanda?

Pack light, breathable clothing in neutral colors, sturdy hiking boots, a waterproof jacket, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, a camera, and any personal medications. Don’t forget a good quality water bottle and a small backpack for day trips.

3. Do I need a visa to visit Rwanda?

Yes, most travelers will need a visa to enter Rwanda. Tourist visas can be obtained upon arrival at Kigali International Airport or online before your trip. It's recommended to check the specific visa requirements based on your nationality.

4. Is gorilla trekking safe in Rwanda?

Yes, gorilla trekking in Rwanda is safe. Treks are conducted by experienced guides and rangers who ensure the safety of visitors while minimizing the impact on the gorillas. Following the guidelines and instructions provided by the guides is essential.

5. How physically demanding is gorilla trekking?

Gorilla trekking can be physically demanding as it involves hiking through dense forests and sometimes steep and uneven terrain. A moderate level of fitness is required, and treks can last anywhere from 1 to 5 hours depending on the location of the gorilla group.

6. How much does a gorilla trekking permit cost?

As of 2024, a gorilla trekking permit in Rwanda costs $1,500 per person. This fee includes the trek, park entry, and an hour with a gorilla family. It's advisable to book permits well in advance due to limited availability.

7. What wildlife can I expect to see in Rwanda besides gorillas?

Besides mountain gorillas, Rwanda offers the chance to see golden monkeys, chimpanzees, a variety of other primates, elephants, lions, leopards, buffalos, hippos, crocodiles, and a wide range of bird species, especially in parks like Akagera and Nyungwe.

8. Is Rwanda safe for tourists?

Rwanda is considered one of the safest countries in Africa for tourists. The country has low crime rates and a stable political environment. However, it's always advisable to follow common travel safety guidelines.

9. What types of accommodation are available on a Rwanda safari?

Rwanda offers a range of accommodations from luxury lodges and boutique hotels to mid-range lodges and budget-friendly options. There are also eco-lodges and tented camps for a more immersive experience.

10. Can I combine a Rwanda safari with visits to other East African countries?

Yes, many travelers combine a Rwanda safari with visits to neighboring countries such as Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzania. East Africa offers diverse safari experiences, and regional flights make it easy to travel between countries.
