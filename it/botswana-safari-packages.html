Botswana Safari Packages

Embarking on a safari in Botswana promises an unforgettable adventure through some of Africa's most pristine and diverse wilderness areas. Renowned for its vast and unspoiled landscapes, abundant wildlife, and commitment to conservation, Botswana offers a safari experience unlike any other.

Okavango Delta: One of Botswana's most iconic safari destinations, the Okavango Delta is a sprawling oasis of waterways, floodplains, and islands that teem with wildlife year-round. Explore this unique ecosystem on mokoro (traditional dugout canoe) excursions, guided bush walks, and game drives, where you'll encounter a wealth of wildlife, including elephants, hippos, crocodiles, and a variety of bird species.

Chobe National Park: Situated in northern Botswana, Chobe National Park is renowned for its dense elephant population, which congregates along the Chobe River during the dry season. Enjoy game drives along the riverbanks, where you'll witness large herds of elephants, as well as lions, buffaloes, and other predators. Chobe is also a prime birdwatching destination, with over 450 bird species recorded within the park.

Moremi Game Reserve: Nestled within the Okavango Delta, Moremi Game Reserve is a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts, boasting a diverse range of habitats, from floodplains and lagoons to woodlands and savannahs. Explore the reserve on game drives and boat safaris, where you'll have the chance to spot predators such as lions, leopards, and wild dogs, as well as antelopes, giraffes, and zebras.

Central Kalahari Game Reserve: For those seeking a more remote and off-the-beaten-path safari experience, the Central Kalahari Game Reserve offers vast expanses of pristine wilderness and the opportunity to encounter desert-adapted wildlife. Explore the reserve on game drives and guided walks, where you'll have the chance to see species such as cheetahs, brown hyenas, and meerkats against the backdrop of the Kalahari Desert.

Savuti: Part of the Chobe National Park, the Savuti region is famous for its predator-rich landscapes and dramatic wildlife encounters. Explore the area on game drives, where you'll have the chance to witness lion prides on the hunt, leopards lurking in the shadows, and hyenas scavenging for food. Savuti is also known for its ancient rock paintings, providing glimpses into the region's rich cultural heritage.

In addition to its spectacular wildlife and landscapes, Botswana is known for its commitment to sustainable tourism and conservation. Many safari lodges and camps in the country operate with low-impact, eco-friendly practices, ensuring that future generations can continue to enjoy the beauty and biodiversity of this remarkable destination. Whether you're a seasoned safari enthusiast or embarking on your first African adventure, a safari in Botswana promises an unforgettable journey into the heart of the wild.

Best Time to Go

The best time to visit Botswana is during the dry season, from May to October. This period is ideal for several reasons.

Wildlife Viewing The dry season offers excellent wildlife viewing opportunities as animals congregate around water sources. The vegetation is also less dense, making it easier to spot wildlife.

Weather The weather is more pleasant with sunny days and cooler nights. Temperatures are typically mild and comfortable, especially from May to August.

Low Malaria Risk The dry season corresponds with a lower risk of malaria, making it a safer time for travel in terms of health.

Peak Safari Experience Most safari lodges and camps operate at full capacity during this time, providing a wide range of activities and services to enhance your safari experience.

Here are some popular questions often asked by travelers planning a safari in Botswana

1. What is the best time of year to go on a safari in Botswana?

The best time to visit Botswana for a safari is during the dry season, from May to October, when wildlife viewing is at its peak.

2. What should I pack for a Botswana safari?

Pack light, breathable clothing in neutral colors, a wide-brimmed hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, a camera, and sturdy walking shoes. Don’t forget any personal medications and a good-quality water bottle.

3. Do I need any vaccinations or medications before traveling to Botswana?

Yes, it's recommended to have vaccinations for hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, and rabies. Malaria prophylaxis is also advised, especially if you are visiting during the wetter months.

4. What type of accommodation is available on a Botswana safari?

Accommodation ranges from luxury lodges and tented camps to mid-range lodges and budget camping options. There are also mobile camps for a more adventurous experience.

5. How safe is it to travel to Botswana for a safari?

Botswana is considered one of the safest countries in Africa for tourists. However, it’s always important to take standard precautions, such as not leaving valuables unattended and following the advice of your guide.

7. What is the typical duration of a Botswana safari?

Most Botswana safaris last between 5 to 10 days, depending on the range of parks and reserves you wish to visit and your overall travel plans.

8. Is it necessary to book a guided tour for a Botswana safari?

While self-drive safaris are possible, a guided tour is highly recommended. Professional guides enhance the experience with their knowledge of wildlife behavior and tracking, and they ensure your safety in remote areas.

9. Can I combine a Botswana safari with visits to other countries?

Yes, many travelers combine a Botswana safari with trips to neighboring countries such as Namibia, Zambia, Zimbabwe (especially for Victoria Falls), and South Africa.

10. What wildlife can I expect to see in Botswana?

Botswana is home to a diverse range of wildlife, including elephants, lions, leopards, cheetahs, rhinos, hippos, crocodiles, zebras, wildebeest, and numerous bird species. The Okavango Delta and Chobe National Park are particularly renowned for their wildlife.
