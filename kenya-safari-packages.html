Going on a safari in Kenya is the dream of many wildlife enthusiasts. Here are some frequently asked questions about safaris in Kenya:

When is the best time to go on a safari in Kenya?

The best time to go on safaris in Kenya is during the dry season, which runs from late June to October and January to February. The dry season coincides with the great migration of animals in the Masai Mara, which usually occurs between July and October.

What are the best national parks and reserves for safaris in Kenya?

The Maasai Mara National Reserve is the most famous, known for its great migration and abundant wildlife. Amboseli National Park offers spectacular views of Mount Kilimanjaro and large elephant herds. Tsavo National Park (Tsavo East and Tsavo West) is one of the largest wildlife reserves in the world. Lake Nakuru National Park is famous for its flamingo and rhino sanctuaries.

What wildlife can I expect to see on a safari in Kenya?

Kenya is home to the "Big Five" (lion, leopard, elephant, buffalo and rhino). Other common wildlife include giraffes, zebras, cheetahs, hippos, crocodiles, wildebeests and a variety of birds.

Is it safe to go on safaris in Kenya?

Safaris in Kenya are generally safe, especially when booked through a reputable tour operator. Visitors should follow the instructions of their safari guide and take standard travel precautions.

What types of accommodation are available at Kenya Safaris?

Accommodation options range from luxury lodges and tent sites to budget-friendly camping sites such as basic campsites and mid-range cabins, and many lodges offer all-inclusive packages that include meals, safaris and other activities.

What do I need to prepare for a safari in Kenya?

Essential items include neutral-colored clothing, comfortable walking shoes, a hat, sunscreen, insect repellent, a camera with spare batteries, binoculars, and any necessary medicines. Dressing in layers is recommended as temperatures can fluctuate greatly.

Do I need any vaccinations or medications before travelling to Kenya?

Vaccinations for yellow fever, typhoid, hepatitis A and B, and tetanus are recommended. Anti-malarial medication is also recommended. Always consult your healthcare provider for the latest health advice before traveling.

How much does a safari in Kenya typically cost?

Costs vary depending on luxury, length of stay, and specific parks visited. Economy safaris start at around $150-250 per day, mid-range safaris are around $250-500 per day, and luxury safaris can cost over $1,000 per day.

Can I combine a safari in Kenya with other activities?

Yes, many travelers combine safaris with other activities, such as a beach holiday in Mombasa or Zanzibar, mountain climbing (such as Mount Kenya), visiting a Maasai village for a cultural tour, or taking a hot air balloon flight over the Maasai Mara.

Any tips for getting the most out of my safari?

Follow your guide's instructions, be patient and observant, respect the wildlife, keep a safe distance, and take your time to enjoy the journey. Don't rush to see all the attractions at once. Early morning and late evening are the best times to see wildlife.
